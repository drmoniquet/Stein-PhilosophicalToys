Stein, “A Transatlantic Interview,” 31.

“…nothing strange a single hurt color and an arrangement in a system to pointing.” Stein, “A Carafe, that is a Blind Glass,” Tender Buttons, 9.

Tender Buttons can be understood as a linguistic experiment stretching the limits of language and the poetic genre, a scientific experiment stretching the limits of empirical observation, and even a psychological experiment in rendering in words the stream of consciousness. However, the poem’s importance comes into focus when it is understood as a radical philosophical experiment that offers a sustained critique of Western epistemology, its foundational postulates (such as knowledge, rationality, certainty, truth) as well as its methods (reasoning, measuring, believing).
